Registering cosmetic products in the UK

For safety purposes all cosmetic products intended for sale or given away in the UK must be registered in the UK.

To register a product you need to give details of:

  • the organistation or individual responsible for ensuring regulations are followed, known as the ‘responsible person’
  • the cosmetic name
  • the formulation
  • an image of the packaging and label

Cosmetic products notified in the EU before 29 March 2019

You can continue to sell or give away cosmetic products that were notified to the European Union (EU) before Brexit. You must provide the following information by 27 June 2019:

  • the UK-based responsible person
  • the cosmetic name
  • the formulation
  • an image of the packaging and label

How to register cosmetic products in the UK

Cosmetic products available in the EU and the UK

If your cosmetic products are on sale in both the EU and UK, you can complete an EU notification first and export the EU notification zip file (not PDF) to make registration in the UK simpler.

You will need to:

  • export all cosmetic products as zip files
  • prepare an image file for each product, this can be the same as you used for the EU notification.
  • if the original notification had attached a document with formulation information, you will also need provide this

Cosmetic products not available in the EU

You can register a cosmetic product by entering information about the product.

You will need:

  • the name
  • the shades, if available in more than 1 shade
  • the size, if available in more than 1 size
  • the ingredients as either a frame formulation, ingredients range, or exact concentrations
  • an image of the packaging and label

If cosmetic the product was not notified in the EU before 29 March 2019, you will be asked to provide information about:

  • carcinogenic, mutagenic or toxic for reproduction substances (CMR)
  • nanomaterials

Cosmetics products containing nanomaterials

If a cosmetic product contains nanomaterials not already approved for use in cosmetics, the responsible person must notify us of their proposed use 6 months before the cosmetic product containing them goes on sale.

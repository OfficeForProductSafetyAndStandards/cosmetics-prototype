Registering cosmetic products in the UK

For safety purposes all cosmetic products on sale in the UK must be registered. To register a product you need to give details of:

  • the organistation responsible for ensuring regulations are followed, known as the ‘responsible person’
  • the cosmetic name, formulation and packaging

Cosmetic products on sale in the EU before Brexit

Cosmetic product notified to the European Union (EU) before Brexit, can continue to be sold in the UK if information about a new UK-based responsible person and the cosmetic formulation is provided within 90 days of the UK leaving the EU.

Read about cosmetics products and Brexit

Cosmetics containing nanomaterials

If a cosmetic contains nanomaterials not already explicitly approved for use in cosmetics, the responsible person must notify the UK government of their proposed use 6 months before the cosmetic containing them goes on sale.

Intended use of nanomaterials that has already been notified to the European Commission does not need to be notified to the UK government, as long as a UK responsible person provides evidence of this notification within 90 days of the UK leaving the European Union.

Cosmetics containing nanomaterials not already explicitly approved for use may go on sale in the UK no sooner than 7 months after the date of notification to the European Commission if this occurred before the UK left the EU, or 6 months after the date of notification to the UK government.

Read about using nanomaterials in cosmetics.

Registering new cosmetic products in the UK

For safety purposes all cosmetic products intended for sale or to be given away in the UK must be registered in the UK.

To register a cosmetic product you need to give details of:

  • the organisation or individual responsible for ensuring regulations are followed, known as the ‘responsible person’
  • a contact person for medical enquiries
  • the cosmetic product name
  • if applicable, the shades the cosmetic product is available in
  • if the cosmetic product contains carcinogenic, mutagenic or toxic for reproduction substances (CMR)
  • if the cosmetic product contains nanomaterials
  • the formulation as either a frame formulation, ranges, or exact concentrations
  • an image of the label

Cosmetic products notified in the EU before 29 March 2019

To continue to sell or give away cosmetic products that were notified to the European Union (EU) before Brexit, you must register your products in the UK within 90 days of the UK leaving the EU – that is 11:00pm on 27 June 2019.

You do not have to register cosmetic products discontinued before 29 March 2019.

You will need to provide details of:

  • the UK-based responsible person
  • a contact person for medical enquiries
  • the cosmetic product name
  • the formulation as either a frame formulation, ranges, or exact concentrations

How to register cosmetic products in the UK

Cosmetic products notified in the EU

The EU Cosmetic Product Notification Portal (CPNP) allows you to export ZIP files containing XML files for each notification.

You can upload notification files only as ZIP files containing XML files on the UK cosmetic product registration service.

To use the data from your EU notifications for UK registration you need to:

  • export your data as ZIP files for all cosmetic products
  • upload your ZIP file containing XML files to the UK service
  • if the original EU notification file had an attached document with formulation information, you will also need add this

Cosmetic products that have not been notified in the EU

You can register a cosmetic product by manually entering information about the product.

Cosmetics products containing nanomaterials

If a cosmetic product contains nanomaterials not already approved for use in cosmetics, the responsible person must notify us of their proposed use 6 months before the cosmetic product containing them goes on sale.
